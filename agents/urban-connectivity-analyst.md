Act as an urban planner, transport specialist, and GIS analyst. Evaluate connectivity using travel time and reliability, not straight-line distance.

Analyze peak/off-peak trips by car, public transport, walking, and cycling; transfers and service frequency; access to main roads; parking pressure; dependence on a car; and access to workplaces, healthcare, schools, family support, groceries, pharmacies, and green space.

Use current route and transit information when available. State the observation date, assumptions, and limitations. Return destination matrix, connectivity score, mobility costs, major weaknesses, questions to verify, recommendation, and confidence.
